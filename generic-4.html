An Exploratory Data Analysis of US Energy Generation by Fuel Source

Data Analysis Portfolio Project

Context

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) is a crucial step in understanding and interpreting large datasets, especially in the energy sector.

For the dataset on U.S. energy generation by fuel type from 2001 to 2024, EDA helps uncover:

Trends Over Time: Visualizing changes in fuel usage (e.g., decline of coal, rise of renewables) highlights how U.S. energy policy, technology, and demand have evolved.
Comparative Insights: EDA enables comparisons across fuel types, helping identify dominant sources (like natural gas) and emerging contributors (like solar and wind).
Correlation Analysis: Understanding relationships between variables (e.g., solar vs. total generation) supports better modeling and forecasting.

Summary Statistics

The describe() function in Python (using pandas) provides a quick statistical summary of numerical columns in a DataFrame. For each column, it outputs key metrics like count, mean, standard deviation, minimum, 25th percentile, median (50%), 75th percentile, and maximum. This is great for a quick overview into the scale of each fuel, detecting anomalies and comparing fuel types.

Visualising trends

Correlation Heatmaps

Correlation quickly reveals which fuel types rise or fall together (e.g., wind + gas) and which move in opposite directions (e.g., coal vs. solar). This helps analysts understand substitution vs. complementarity in the grid. A heat‑map offers an instant visual cue — a quicker glance where relationships are strongest or weakest without wading through tables of numbers.

Identifying the most and least correlated energy fuels

  • Renewables Rise, Fossils Fall: Clear trend of negative correlation between fossil fuels and renewables (Coal–Wind: -0.9816), (Coal–Natural Gas: -0.9759) (Coal–Solar: ~-0.90) suggests a long-term shift in U.S. energy policy or infrastructure investment.
  • Complementarity Within Clean Energy sources: Strong positive correlations among renewables (Wind-Natural Gas: 0.9799) and (Geothermal-Natural Gas: 0.8649) imply coordinated growth and push for cleaner energy sources.
  • Solar's growing importance in the energy mix: A correlation of 0.7553 with 'All fuels' implies that solar's contribution to total energy is both substantial and growing consistently over the years. It’s not the dominant source, but its trend aligns with total growth.

    • Overall these trends are useful for forecasting future energy generation mix in the US.

Most and least volatile fuels

The “most” and “least” volatile fuel statistics indicates which fuels swing wildly and which are steady. This lets planners blend assets to reduce overall grid stability, investment risk, and operational flexibility—key ingredients for an optimized, resilient energy mix. The coefficient of variation (CV) is a powerful metric for comparing relative variability across fuels—especially when their average generation levels differ drastically.
